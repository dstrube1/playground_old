General Education Program -
The Common Core for Baccalaureate Programs

The principal purpose of the general education program at Kennesaw State University is to provide for students, regardless of their major, a broad and interdisciplinary foundation of general knowledge skills, and understandings which the faculty of the university consider important for success in the 21st century. Whereas major programs give students essential depth in specific specializations, the general education program provides them with fundamental breadth in their college education. To provide this breadth, approximately one-third of the courses required in a KSU bachelor's degree program are devoted to the enhancement of general education, with each of the courses meeting a clearly defined learning objective.

Listed below are the primary educational objectives and their associated course requirements in the general education program at KSU. Students with advanced standing credits or transfer credits from general education programs at any other institutions may be permitted to satisfy KSU requirements through acceptable course substitutions or the completion of approved general education requirements elsewhere in the University System of Georgia. Students seeking such substitutions or equivalencies should contact the Dean of General Education.

In addition to these primary educational outcomes which are specific to individual courses, the general education program is designed to achieve other cross-disciplinary educational objectives which are shared by many courses in the program.

  • Strengthen understanding of and critical thinking about world social issues
  • Strengthen understanding of cultural, racial, and gender diversity
  • Strengthen understanding of connections among disciplines
  • Strengthen understanding of international and global perspectives
  • Strengthen skills in critical thinking and analysis
  • Strengthen skills in problem solving
  • Strengthen skills in collaboration
  • Strengthen skills in applying concepts and theories to real life situations
  • Strengthen skills in written communication
  • Strengthen skills in oral proficiency and presentation
  • Strengthen skills in reading and comprehension
  • Strengthen skills in use of technology
